Rules

The universe of the Game of Life is an infinite, two-dimensional orthogonal grid of square cells. Each cell is in one of two possible states: alive or dead (also called populated or unpopulated).

Every cell interacts with its eight neighbors: the cells that are horizontally, vertically, or diagonally adjacent. At each step in time, the following transitions occur:

  • Any live cell with fewer than two live neighbors dies, as if by solitude.
  • Any live cell with more than three live neighbors dies, as if by overpopulation.
  • Any live cell with two or three live neighbors lives on to the next generation.
  • Any dead cell with exactly three live neighbors becomes a live cell, as if by reproduction.
